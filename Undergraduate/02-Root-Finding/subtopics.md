# Unit 2: Root Finding

## Subtopics

1. **The Root-Finding Problem**
   - What does it mean to "find a root"?
   - Graphical intuition
   - Why exact solutions are often impossible

2. **The Bisection Method**
   - Intermediate Value Theorem as the foundation
   - Algorithm step-by-step
   - Guaranteed convergence and its rate
   - Strengths and limitations

3. **Fixed-Point Iteration**
   - Reformulating f(x)=0 as x=g(x)
   - The idea of iterative refinement
   - Convergence conditions (contraction mapping, intuitively)
   - Graphical interpretation

4. **Newton's Method (Newton-Raphson)**
   - Derivation from the tangent line idea
   - Algorithm and convergence (quadratic)
   - When it fails: pitfalls and sensitivity to initial guess
   - The role of the derivative

5. **The Secant Method**
   - Approximating the derivative
   - Algorithm and convergence rate
   - Comparison with Newton's method

6. **Comparison and Practical Considerations**
   - Trade-offs: speed vs. reliability
   - Stopping criteria
   - Choosing the right method for the job
